Labor is likely to win. But a 91% chance to win isn't guaranteed. Coalition has a better chance to win the election than one in twenty. This number is generated by a number of factors, all of which have some degree of uncertainty. The more uncertainty the harder it is to make predictions.

A hung parliament is possible. A hung parliament occurs when neither party has a majority. If Labor or the Coalition are left with less the 76 seats after the election, they will have to scramble to form a minority government. The last time this happened was 2010. The model gives this a 7% chance to happen.

The model asks what if the election were held today It's hard enough to guage the current mood of the electorate using polls and other data. The model takes the polls as snapshots of how voters are leaning and asks what if they voted today? The model does not try to predict the future. Anything could still happen in the next little while before the election. The polls could get narrower or further apart. This is not a crystal ball.
